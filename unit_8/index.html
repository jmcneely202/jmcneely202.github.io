About M.O.R.S.

Our goal is simple: To provide readers a pragmatic approach to life's complexities. Many working professionals devote time and money to increasing their professional skills, but lack a basic understanding of how the world operates outside of their jobs. Whether it is explaining the process for applying for a mortgage, or the pros and cons of purchasing whole-life versus term-life insurance, having multiple perspectives when grappling with these decisions is helpful to finding what works best for an individual circumstance. The MORS Team realizes that information is useless without clear explanation and context. Our webiste is dedicated to the proposition that the better informed our readers are, the better their outcomes. We make complex subjects relevant and empower our readers to apply this knowledge to their daily lives.

Topics covered by the MORS team span a wide variety of subject areas, including, but not limited to:

  • Law and Politics
  • Current Events
  • Lifestyle and Fitness
  • Business, Financial Planning, and Real Estate
  • Travel and Leisure
  • Sports and Entertainment

Our Staff

McNeely

Ms. McNeely received her Bachelors of Arts degree in Business Administration and Management from Georgia State University. She later went on to acquire her law degree from the University of Georgia School of Law. Ms. McNeely was active in UGA Law’s advocacy programs, competing as member of the moot court, mock trial and negotiation teams. Ms. McNeely served as president of UGA Law’s Federalist Society and secretary of the Black Law Student Association.

Opara

Ms. Opara earned her Bachelors of Arts degrees in Psychology and Sociology and minored in Policy Journalism and Media Studies at Duke University. Ms. Opara went on to acquire her law degree at Emory University School of Law in 2014, where she was a member of the Emory Law Mock Trial Society and the Frederick Douglass Moot Court Team. After hours, she enjoys traveling, spending time with her family, and engaging in community service activities.

Reed

Ms. Reed earned her law degree from Emory University where she was a founding member of the Emory Corporate Governance & Accountability Review. Before attending Emory, Ms. Reed studied rhetoric and composition at Auburn University. She taught English composition to non-native English speakers at Auburn for four years. After hours, Ms. Reed enjoys sailing, photography, and spending time with her family.

Stewart

Ms. Stewart worked as an information technology professional supporting the corporate infrastructure in various industries. Prior to joining the firm, Ms. Stewart clerked for the Honorable Kimberly Esmond Adams in the Superior Court of Fulton County. In her spare time, Ms. Stewart enjoys running and motorcycling.
